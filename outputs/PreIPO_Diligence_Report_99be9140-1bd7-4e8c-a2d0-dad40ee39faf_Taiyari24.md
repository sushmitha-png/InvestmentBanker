**MEMORANDUM**

**TO:** Investment Committee
**FROM:** Senior Investment Advisor
**DATE:** 26 May 2024
**SUBJECT:** **CRITICAL DILIGENCE REPORT: Project Edify (Pre-IPO EdTech Platform)**

This report provides a critical assessment of the proposed ₹20.0 Cr primary investment for a 15.0% stake in an unnamed EdTech platform. The analysis is based strictly on the limited information provided in the data pack. Significant information gaps preclude a definitive investment recommendation at this stage.

### 1. Executive Verdict & Conviction

**Verdict: HOLD. Do Not Proceed.**

**Conviction: Very Low.**

The proposed investment is fundamentally non-diligenceable with the information provided. The data pack suffers from a complete absence of financial metrics, management background, and strategic plans. Most critically, the provided market comparables (Healthcare Delivery) are entirely irrelevant to an EdTech business, rendering any valuation discussion invalid.

Proceeding would be an act of speculation, not investing. The implied post-money valuation of ₹133.3 Cr is untethered to any discernible performance metric. This proposal should be rejected until a complete and accurate data room is made available.

### 2. Investment Thesis – what MUST go right

Given the lack of data, we cannot validate a thesis. Instead, we must define the critical pillars upon which a viable thesis would have to be built:

1.  **Demonstrable Product-Market Fit:** The company must prove that its AI-powered platform delivers a quantifiable ROI to academic institutions, evidenced by high renewal rates and willingness to pay, not just free trials.
2.  **Scalable B2B2C Adoption:** The model's success hinges on a dual-pronged sales motion: effectively selling to slow-moving academic institutions (B2B) and subsequently driving high engagement and value for the end-user students (B2C). Both must fire in unison.
3.  **Superior Employment Outcomes:** The core value proposition of making students "employment-ready" must be backed by data. The company must track and demonstrate a statistically significant improvement in placement rates, starting salaries, or time-to-employment for its users versus a control group.
4.  **Effective Monetization:** The company must establish a clear and defensible pricing model (e.g., per-student-per-year fee, platform license) that can scale and lead to high-margin, recurring revenue streams.

Without evidence for these four points, there is no investment thesis.

### 3. Financial Quality & Sustainability Analysis

**Assessment: Impossible to conduct.**

The data pack contains zero financial information (Revenue, EBITDA, Margins). This is a fatal flaw in any pre-IPO diligence process. A full quality of earnings analysis is required, focusing on:

*   **Revenue Recognition:** Is revenue recognized upfront or over the contract term? What is the policy for multi-year contracts?
*   **Customer Concentration:** What percentage of revenue comes from the top 5 or 10 institutions?
*   **Cohort Analysis:** What are the net revenue retention (NRR) and gross revenue retention (GRR) rates for customer cohorts? This is the single most important metric for a B2B platform business.
*   **Unit Economics:** What is the Lifetime Value (LTV) to Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) ratio? A healthy SaaS business typically targets a ratio >3x.

Without this data, we cannot assess the quality, predictability, or sustainability of the business.

### 4. Growth Quality & Operating Leverage Assessment

**Assessment: Unverifiable.**

The provided analytics state "Moderate" operating leverage but correctly note that there is insufficient data to support this. While a platform business model *should* exhibit operating leverage as it scales, we have no evidence this is the case.

To assess growth quality, we require:

*   **Historical Revenue CAGR:** To understand the growth trajectory and momentum.
*   **Sales Efficiency Metrics:** How long is the sales cycle? What is the payback period on CAC?
*   **Source of Growth:** Is growth coming from new logos, expansion within existing accounts (upsell/cross-sell), or price increases? A healthy mix is ideal.

The current lack of data prevents any analysis of whether growth is sustainable, profitable, or capital-efficient.

### 5. Valuation & Multiple Justification

**Assessment: Fundamentally Flawed and Unjustifiable.**

The proposed deal structure (₹20.0 Cr for 15.0%) implies a **post-money valuation of ₹133.3 Cr**.

The provided comparables (Apollo Hospitals, Max Healthcare, etc.) and their median multiple of 23.8x EV/EBITDA are for the **Healthcare Delivery** sector. Applying these to an EdTech company is professionally negligent and indicates a profound lack of understanding of either the target company or valuation principles.

A proper valuation would require:
1.  **Relevant EdTech Comps:** Public companies like Coursera, Chegg, or regional players in the EdTech space.
2.  **Relevant Multiples:** EV/Forward Revenue or EV/ARR are standard for high-growth, pre-profitability tech companies. EV/EBITDA is irrelevant if EBITDA is negative or negligible.
3.  **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis:** Based on a credible, management-backed financial forecast.

As it stands, the ₹133.3 Cr valuation is a "black box" with no justification. We cannot determine if it is reasonable, exorbitant, or cheap.

### 6. Capital Structure & Balance Sheet Risk

**Assessment: Unknown.**

The data pack provides no information on the company's balance sheet, specifically Net Debt.

Key diligence questions that remain unanswered:
*   **Existing Debt:** Does the company have any existing debt? If so, what are the terms, covenants, and maturity dates?
*   **Use of Proceeds:** The ₹20.0 Cr is a primary infusion. How will this capital be deployed? (e.g., product development, sales & marketing expansion, hiring, geographic expansion).
*   **Cash Burn & Runway:** What is the current monthly cash burn rate? How many months of runway will this new capital provide?

Without this information, we cannot assess leverage risk or the capital efficiency of the business.

### 7. Business Model Robustness (what breaks under stress)

The B2B2C model has inherent fragilities:

*   **Institutional Sales Cycles:** Academic institutions are notoriously slow to adopt new technology, with procurement cycles often lasting 12-18 months and being subject to annual budget approvals. An economic downturn could lead to frozen EdTech budgets.
*   **Student Engagement Risk:** Even if an institution pays for the platform, the model fails if students do not actively use it. Low engagement devalues the proposition and leads to churn at renewal.
*   **Competition:** The "skills-gap" market is crowded. The company competes with giants like LinkedIn Learning, Coursera for Campus, and numerous specialized startups. The model breaks if a competitor offers a "good enough" solution at a lower price or for free.
*   **Proving ROI:** The model's core promise is better job outcomes. If the company cannot definitively prove this link with data, its value proposition collapses, and it becomes a "nice-to-have" tool rather than a "must-have" solution.

### 8. Execution & Expansion Risk

**Assessment: High and Unquantifiable.**

The lack of information on both the management team and the expansion plan makes this a primary area of concern.

*   **Management Quality:** We have no information on the founders or key executives. Do they have experience in EdTech, B2B sales, and scaling a technology platform? A pre-IPO investment is largely a bet on the team's ability to execute. This is a blind bet.
*   **Expansion Plan:** No plan is specified. This is a major red flag. Are they planning geographic, product, or vertical expansion? Each carries distinct risks. Without a plan, we cannot assess their strategic thinking or our capital's intended use. Execution risk is, by definition, infinite without a defined plan to execute against.

### 9. Governance & IPO Readiness

**Assessment: Assumed to be Low.**

For a company at the pre-IPO stage, we would expect to see the foundations of public-company governance. The lack of basic financial data suggests these are likely absent. Key areas for immediate diligence would include:

*   **Board Composition:** Is there an independent board of directors?
*   **Audit & Reporting:** Are financials audited by a reputable firm? Do they have the systems to close books on a quarterly basis under IndAS/IFRS?
*   **Key Hires:** Is there a qualified CFO and General Counsel in place?
*   **Capitalization Table:** A clean and well-documented cap table is essential.

The company is likely years away from being IPO-ready from a governance and compliance perspective.

### 10. Downside Scenarios & Fragility Analysis

*   **Scenario 1: Key Client Churn.** The company loses one of its first major university clients after a 1-2 year contract, citing low student engagement or lack of budget. This creates a negative reference case, stalling the sales pipeline.
*   **Scenario 2: Competitive Squeeze.** A large, well-funded competitor (e.g., Coursera, upGrad) launches a similar, more integrated offering for institutions, effectively commoditizing the company's value proposition.
*   **Scenario 3: "Feature, Not a Platform."** The market perceives the solution as a set of useful but non-essential features (resume builder, mock interviews) rather than an integrated platform, leading to an inability to command premium pricing and high churn.
*   **Scenario 4: Cash Burn Crisis.** Aggressive spending on sales and marketing fails to shorten the long institutional sales cycle, leading to a rapid depletion of the new capital before significant revenue traction is achieved.

### 11. Strategic Optionality (IPO vs M&A vs delay)

*   **IPO:** A highly unlikely option in the next 2-3 years. The company needs to demonstrate a clear path to significant scale (e.g., >₹100 Cr in ARR), profitability, and establish public-company-grade governance.
*   **M&A:** The most plausible exit path. The company could be an attractive acquisition target for a larger, global EdTech player looking to enter the market or acquire specific technology/AI capabilities and a foothold in academic institutions.
*   **Delay / Further Private Rounds:** This is the most probable near-term path. The company will need to use this capital to hit critical milestones to justify a larger Series B/C round at a significant valuation step-up.

### 12. Key Red Flags (explicit, no soft language)

1.  **Complete Absence of Financial Data:** No revenue, no costs, no cash flow. This is a non-starter.
2.  **Irrelevant and Misleading Comparables:** Using Healthcare comps for an EdTech firm is a sign of either incompetence or deliberate obfuscation.
3.  **Untethered Valuation:** The ₹133.3 Cr valuation is derived from the deal terms, not from any business metric. It is entirely unsubstantiated.
4.  **No Information on Management:** We are being asked to back a team whose identity, experience, and capabilities are unknown.
5.  **No Stated Use of Proceeds or Strategy:** We do not know what our capital will be used for or what the company's growth plan is.

### 13. 5-Year Outlook (Bear / Base / Bull)

*   **Bear Case:** The company fails to convert its pipeline, student adoption remains low, and it churns its initial customers. It struggles to raise follow-on capital and is either wound down or acquired for its technology/team at a valuation below our entry point.
*   **Base Case:** The company secures a niche with a specific type of academic institution (e.g., private engineering colleges). Growth is steady but not explosive. It achieves modest revenue (e.g., ₹30-50 Cr) but faces intense competition, limiting margin expansion. It raises another round of capital but the exit path remains an M&A at a modest multiple.
*   **Bull Case:** The platform becomes the category-defining solution for skills-based education in its target geography. It demonstrates clear, data-backed employment outcomes, driving high NRR (>120%). It successfully expands its product suite and scales revenue past ₹200 Cr with improving operating leverage. Becomes a prime candidate for a strategic acquisition by a global player or a successful IPO.
